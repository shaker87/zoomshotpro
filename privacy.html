California Online Privacy Protection Act

Caloppa is the State law in the US, which obligates commercial websites and online services to publicly disclose data protection guidelines. This law extends beyond California and requires that a website owner in the United States (potentially also worldwide) whose website raises personal data from Californian consumers, needs to publish a visible privacy statement on his website on which it is clearly described which data is collected and who has access to it.
